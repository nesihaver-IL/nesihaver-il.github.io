Insights

What the data says

Not averages. Not trends. The story behind the numbers.

Origin
Born in a lockdown
Training started in May 2020, when gyms closed and the street was the only option. Indoor running, a Garmin on the wrist, nothing else to measure. The lockdown ended. The streak did not.
Six years from that first session — still going.
Identity
The swimmer
Of 830 recorded sessions, 511 happened in a pool. That is 62 percent. Swimming is not a secondary sport — it is the backbone of the whole thing. Running fills the outdoor days. Cycling adds the distance. But the pool is where it all starts each week.

850 kilometres covered in 25-metre lanes. That is the width of France, end to end, in chlorinated water.
Nothing else comes close.
Peak
2021 — the year it clicked
The first full calendar year brought 152 sessions — almost once every two days, for twelve months straight. That was not an accident. 2021 was the year the habit became automatic. Every year since has used it as the reference point.
152 is still the number to beat.
Still Moving
Year six, no signs of slowing
2026 is 39 sessions old through April. The pace PR came in March. The longer runs started in December. At 47, the body is logging personal bests — not in spite of the years of training, but because of them. Consistency compounds differently than intensity.
The best records in this dataset are from the last six months.
